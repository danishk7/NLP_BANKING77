# NLP_BANKING77
This study comprehends an end-to-end NLP pipeline designed specifically for banking chatbot development, evaluated on the BANKING77  dataset developed by PolyAI (Casanueva et al., 2020) and is available under a CC BY 4.0 licence. It contains 13,083 annotated customer service queries across 77 fine-grained banking intent categories, and a sample of 2000 gets selected to perform the test due to resource limitations.
And is composed of five tasks. (1) NLTK and spaCy process tokenisation, lemmatisation, stopword removal, and named entity recognition alongside exploratory data analysis that characterises the dataset's vocabulary and class distribution. (2) constructs TF-IDF feature representations and evaluates three classical classifiers: logistic regression, Naive Bayes, and linear SVM, establishing quantitative performance baselines. (3) trains a Word2Vec Skip-Gram semantic embedding model on the BANKING77 corpus, visualises the resulting embedding space, and demonstrates a retrieval-based chatbot prototype grounded in cosine similarity. (4) fine-tunes DistilBERT for three epochs on the intent classification task, achieving substantially superior performance over classical baselines on both accuracy and macro-F1 metrics. (5) analyses deployment of conversational AI in banking environments and identifies failure under various conditions.
